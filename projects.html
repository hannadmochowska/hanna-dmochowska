Closed-Loop Auditory Stimulation (CLAS) — SWS Phase Targeting

Literature-driven methods review of CLAS during slow-wave sleep (SWS/N3). Auditory tones are phase-locked to the slow-oscillation (SO) up-state to boost SO amplitude, increase spindle density, and strengthen SO–spindle coupling—the mechanisms most consistently linked to improved declarative memory. Effects on procedural tasks are mixed and appear stage/mechanism-specific. Precise timing and inter-individual sleep architecture strongly modulate efficacy.

  • Band-pass 0.5–1.5 Hz · Hilbert phase · latency compensation
  • Outputs: phase-error distribution (°), jitter, robustness vs SNR

Transcriptomic Differences Between HER2-Amplified and Non-Amplified Breast Tumours in TCGA-BRCA

A differential expression and pathway analysis of HER2-amplified vs. non-amplified breast tumours using TCGA-BRCA RNA-seq data. The analysis identified ~9,700 DE genes, with HER2+ tumours showing strong mitotic/cell-cycle upregulation and suppression of luminal differentiation and RNA-surveillance pathways. Pathway enrichment highlighted coordinated activation of DNA replication and checkpoint machinery, consistent with known HER2-driven proliferative signalling. A LASSO Cox model using the top DEGs revealed a small subset of genes with prognostic associations.

  • Methods: DESeq2 · VST/PCA · ReactomePA/clusterProfiler · LASSO Cox
  • Outputs: DEGs · pathway enrichment · PCA/heatmap · survival model

Aperiodic Spectral Slope Pipeline (REM)

Pipeline for estimating the aperiodic (1/f) slope during REM from hippocampal LFP. Uses Welch power spectra and (optionally) FOOOF to separate periodic peaks from the aperiodic background. Includes scripts for parameter reporting and reproducible fits.

  • Data scope: REM-labeled segments; hippocampal recordings
  • Methods: Welch PSD → aperiodic slope (log–log fit or FOOOF)
  • Analyses: RGS14 control vs positive; OS vs Homecage (exploratory)
  • Findings: trends toward flatter slopes in RGS14-positive and OS, not statistically significant
